MixtureDataset normalizes the weights into a probability distribution and uses np.random.choice to pick which dataset to draw from at each step. So [1.0, 1.0] → [0.5, 0.5] — every sample has a 50/50 chance of coming from cc12m vs things, regardless of the actual sizes of the two datasets.

Concretely with your data: - cc12m has 53 shards, things has 27 shards - With equal weights, roughly half your training tokens come from things even though it's the smaller dataset

If you wanted to weight proportionally to dataset size you'd use [53, 27] (or [2.0, 1.0] approximately). Equal weights as you have them is a deliberate choice to oversample things — which makes sense for the neural condition since that's where your neural data lives.
