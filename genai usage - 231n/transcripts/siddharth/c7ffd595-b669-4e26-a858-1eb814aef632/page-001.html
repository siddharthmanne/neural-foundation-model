1	# MEG tokenization — diagnostics and findings
2	
3	Brief reference for what we've measured on the BrainOmni 3b tokenizer, how the eval was hardened against false positives/negatives, and the experimental grid that backs the verdict. See [neural_tokenizers/meg/CLAUDE.md §9](../neural_tokenizers/meg/CLAUDE.md) for the production leaderboard; [linear_probe_design.md](linear_probe_design.md) for probe rationale.
4	
5	## TL;DR
6	
7	**BrainOmni 3b's tokens preserve SUBJECT identity richly but lose OBJECT CATEGORY structure entirely.** Same tokens, three probe families. CNN → tokens decode subject at **97%** (vs 25% chance, near-perfect) but category at **3.6%** (vs 3.7% chance, at floor). The story isn't "tokens are bad" — it's selective: bits got spent on the signal that dominates variance (subject-level / sensor-level features), the small-amplitude category signal got dropped.
8	
9	Raw MEG behaves the same way (98% subject vs 4.2% category with CNN) — the asymmetry is intrinsic to the data, not the tokenizer. Tokens preserve roughly the subject-level information present in raw; they don't preserve category info, but raw barely has it linearly decodable either.
10

12

39	## CNN probe head — design
40	
41	The flat MLP probe has no inductive bias for the structure that THINGS-MEG decoding actually exploits (Cichy / Hebart papers: channel-topographic patterns over time). A 2-layer MLP on flattened tokens may simply lack the right shape; per Dixen 2024 even on EEG, EEGNet/Conformer-style temporal convolutions massively outperform flat heads at this SNR.
42	
43	The new `cnn` head exploits the token tensor's natural structure rather than flattening it:
44	
45	- **For BrainOmni tokens** of shape `(B, 16 latents, 8 time, D=512 embed)`: a small **2-D CNN** with conv over `(latent × time)` and `D` as channels. Two conv blocks with batch-norm + ReLU + global average pooling → linear classifier. ~150k params.
46	- **For raw MEG** of shape `(B, 271 channels, 281 time)`: a small **1-D CNN** over time, channels as input dims. Two strided conv blocks → global average pooling → linear. ~50k params.
47	- **For random tokens**: same shape transform as `tokens` (random codes → `tokens_to_embedding` → CNN). Tests "what does the CNN do with uninformative tokens of the right shape?".
48	- **For μ-transform-style position-preserving tokens** `(B, 1, channels × time)`: reshape to `(B, channels, time)` then use the 1-D CNN — equivalent to applying the raw-MEG CNN to the dequantized signal.
49	
50	Rationale: small enough to not overfit on ~4k training trials per fold, while giving the probe the same inductive bias (temporal smoothness + latent-position locality) that real MEG decoders use.

143	## §5.3 probe hardening (timeline)
144	
145	| Iteration | What we added | Why |
146	|---|---|---|
147	| v0 | Linear head, top-1 + top-5, single split | Baseline §5.3 contract |
148	| v1 | Label space → THINGS **27-way superordinate** (was 1854-way concept) | 2.6 trials/class is statistically dead; 27-way → ~185/class |
149	| v2 | **5-fold CV**, mean ± std | Single-split was un-error-barred |
150	| v3 | **Class-weighted CE** | Unweighted CE on imbalanced labels collapses to majority prediction |
151	| v4 | **Balanced accuracy** as the headline metric | Top-1 floor with imbalance is `max(class_freq)`, not 1/n_classes |
152	| v5 | **2-layer MLP** option (`probe_classifier="mlp"`) | Catches "info is there but nonlinear" |
153	| v6 | **Per-RVQ-layer probing** (`tokens_rvq0` vs `tokens_all`) | Asks whether coarse layer alone carries info |
154	| v7 | **Position-preserving `tokens_to_embedding`** for μ-transform | BoC was discarding the position μ-transform faithfully preserves |
155	| v8 | **3 label spaces** (`category27`/`animacy`/`subject`) | Subject is pipeline sanity check (Dixen 2024 trick); animacy is sensitivity check |
156	| v9 | **§5.5 retrieval axis** (cosine prec@K + Jaccard) | Zero-trainable probe: separates "tokens bad" from "classifier bad" |
157	| **v10** | **CNN probe head** (`probe_classifier="cnn"`) | Right inductive bias for temporal-spatial structure; Dixen 2024 shows architecture matters at this SNR |

109	    X: torch.Tensor | np.ndarray,
110	    image_ids: np.ndarray,
111	) -> tuple[torch.Tensor, np.ndarray]:
112	    """Group trials by `image_id` and average within each group.
113	
114	    Produces one signal per unique image — the canonical "cross-subject +
115	    cross-rep" averaging used by Experiment 2 (see
116	    ``notes/meg_tokenization.md``). The averaging axis collapses both
117	    subject and within-subject repetitions, so the output has no subject
118	    label per row.
119	
120	    Args:
121	        X: ``(N, C, T)`` torch or numpy array of single-trial signals.
122	        image_ids: ``(N,)`` int array, trial-aligned image codes.
123	
124	    Returns:
125	        X_avg:     ``(M, C, T)`` torch.float32 tensor (M = unique image
126	                   count).
127	        image_ids_unique: ``(M,)`` int64 numpy array, sorted ascending.
128	
129	    Memory-frugal: accumulates in float64 element-wise inside `np.add.at`
130	    but **never materializes a float64 copy of the input**. The 88k-trial
131	    training tensor (~27 GB float32) is the largest object in this
132	    pipeline; doubling it with an f64 cast would push the container OOM.
133	    """
134	    if isinstance(X, torch.Tensor):
135	        X_np = X.detach().cpu().numpy()  # no dtype cast — preserve source f32
136	    else:
137	        X_np = np.asarray(X)
138	
139	    image_ids = np.asarray(image_ids, dtype=np.int64)
140	    if X_np.shape[0] != image_ids.shape[0]:
141	        raise ValueError(
142	            f"X.shape[0]={X_np.shape[0]} must match image_ids length "
143	            f"{image_ids.shape[0]}"
144	        )
145

147	    n_groups = len(unique_ids)
148	    C, T = X_np.shape[1], X_np.shape[2]
149	
150	    # Sums stay in f64 for accumulator precision when a single group has
151	    # 40+ contributors (test images × 4 subjects × 12 reps); numpy promotes
152	    # f32 source elements element-wise inside np.add.at — no global cast.
153	    sums = np.zeros((n_groups, C, T), dtype=np.float64)
154	    counts = np.zeros(n_groups, dtype=np.int64)
155	    np.add.at(sums, inverse, X_np)
156	    np.add.at(counts, inverse, 1)
157
